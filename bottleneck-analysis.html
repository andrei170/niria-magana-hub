Bottleneck 1 · Three-vertical mentorship has no standalone surface; lives inside someone else's Skool

Snapshot

You currently mentor across tax (7 active mentees), credit repair, and now Airbnb investing. The mentorship piggybacks inside your own mentor's Skool community - not a standalone Wealthy Beginnings surface. The architecture works for the relationship you have with your mentor, but it caps the brand. New mentees discover the borrowed Skool, not Wealthy Beginnings. The 30% rev-share on mentees' tax filings flows fine, but a prospect who finds you on IG has no Wealthy-Beginnings-branded mentorship page to land on.

What you described wanting to build: tax + credit + Airbnb under one umbrella, with the $25K tier as the anchor, the $5K-front-and-back high-earner model layered in, the $99/mo or one-time firm-license entry rung visible, and a standalone Wealthy Beginnings community that you own.

Asymmetry

You have:

  • 14+ years of operator credibility in tax prep
  • 7 tax preppers as a labor force you can deploy on mentee client work
  • 7 active mentees inside the tax mentorship already
  • A named mentee win (Nikki: $40-50K credit repair + $15K taxes, while pregnant)
  • Personal investment in high-earner tax-planning education ($5K with your own mentor) that translates into the $30-50K-per-high-earner saving frame
  • An Airbnb investment that gives you a third teachable vertical
  • A 30% rev-share alignment model already running across your existing mentees

What you don't have:

  • A Wealthy-Beginnings-owned mentorship surface where the three-vertical mentorship is public, buyable, and discoverable

Cost (Year-1 floor, conservative)

A standalone three-vertical mentorship anchored on the $25K top tier with the $5K-front-and-back high-earner model as the second tier, capturing even 4-6 new mentees Year-1 across the two upper tiers, plus the recurring 30% rev-share on those mentees' client filings:

  • 2-3 new top-tier mentees Year-1: $50K-$75K
  • 4-6 new high-earner-tier mentees Year-1 at the $5K front-end: $20K-$30K
  • 20% backend on the high-earner mentees' filings, conservative book of 4-6 high-earner clients per mentee: $20K-$50K rolling
  • 30% rev-share on existing + new tax-mentee filings (your existing alignment model, captured cleanly on the standalone surface): $30K-$80K

Year-1 floor on this bottleneck alone, conservative: $120K-$235K.

Fix

Standalone Wealthy Beginnings mentorship surface. Hero positions the three-vertical mentorship (tax + credit + Airbnb). Tier ladder visible. Application gate. CROA + IRS Circular 230 + state tax-preparer disclaimers in the footer. Spun out of the borrowed Skool with continuity for existing mentees.

Impact

Highest leverage. The other four bottlenecks route here. This is the unblock the rest of the funnel depends on.

Why this is #1

You already have the credibility, the labor force, the mentees, the case study, and the rev-share alignment. What is missing is the surface. The surface is a sub-week build. The surface unlocks the ladder, the seasonality logic, and the LinkedIn distribution play.

Bottleneck 2 · Tier ladder exists in conversation, not on a page

Snapshot

You described the tiers clearly on the WhatsApp call:

  • $60K-and-under income clients - basic training tier (you teach mentees how to serve this segment)
  • High earners - premium tier; you save them $30-50K per year in taxes via the high-earner planning playbook
  • High-earner "sauced up return" - structured as front-end + backend (20% backend is the existing alignment)
  • Top mentorship tier - your current ceiling for one-on-one + cohort-room mentorship
  • Software-license tier - monthly subscription OR one-time firm-license for mentees who want to launch their own tax firm

That ladder exists in conversation. None of it sits on a public page. A prospect cannot self-select. A high earner who finds you on LinkedIn has no obvious entry point. A mentee considering the firm-license has no public surface to land on. The cohort fills slower because the path looks vague from the outside.

Asymmetry

You have a fully-formed ladder with five distinct tiers and a 30% rev-share alignment on the mentee tier. What is missing is the public page that lays it out. Once a prospect sees "the ladder," conversion friction drops at every rung. The high-earner self-selects into the $5K front-end. The aspiring firm-owner self-selects into the software-license. The committed operator self-selects into the top tier.

Cost (Year-1 floor)

Without a visible ladder, the cohort and top-tier capture is gated by sales conversation friction. With a visible ladder:

  • Software-license tier captures 6-10 firm-launches Year-1 (warm-list + IG referrals + LinkedIn): $9K-$18K direct on the one-time fee; recurring on the subscription path
  • High-earner $5K front-end captures 6-10 placements Year-1: $30K-$50K direct; backend rolls in over months
  • The cohort and top-tier conversion lift from ladder visibility: 1-2 extra placements at the top tier per year

Year-1 floor on this bottleneck: $45K-$100K direct plus indirect lift on Bottleneck 1's tier.

Fix

Ladder visible on the standalone mentorship landing. Anchors held private (no exact dollar amounts published). Public language uses ranges and tier names. Application gate at every rung. Calendly behind the application for the top two tiers.

Impact

Converts the warm list. Filters the cold audience. Makes every rung self-selecting. Removes the bottleneck of "which tier is right for me" being a sales conversation rather than a self-serve decision.

Why this is #2

The tiers exist. The audience exists. The page is missing. This is a one-week build that unlocks 2-3x conversion lift on the cohort and top-tier rungs.

Bottleneck 3 · Seasonality-aware funnel: one funnel for Q1 mommy season, a different funnel for Q2-Q4 high earners

Snapshot

Your business runs on a sharp seasonal cadence:

  • Q1 (January, February, March) - mommy season. Cash earners with kids. $10-13K refunds typical. Highest revenue months. The message is "let's get you your refund."
  • March-April - transition. You start the pivot to high earners. The message shifts from refunds to tax planning.
  • Q2-Q4 (May through December) - high earners, amendments, prior year returns, estimated tax planning. The message is "do you owe money this year?" and "save $30-50K next year."

A single funnel cannot serve both audiences. The mommy-season audience wants the refund and the path off the W-2. The high-earner audience wants the tax savings and the planning. Two ICPs. Two messages. Two funnel surfaces. Today, you have one (the existing brand site, primarily credit-facing).

Asymmetry

You already know the calendar. You already know the two audiences. You already serve both. What is missing is two parallel funnel surfaces - one Q1-mommy-season lead-magnet flow ("Mommy Refund Checklist" or similar), one Q2-Q4 high-earner flow ("High Earner Tax Savings Audit"). Each routes to the appropriate mentorship rung.

Cost (Year-1 floor)

The Q1 mommy-season funnel captures aspiring credit-repair operators and tax-prep entrepreneurs - the next-Nikki audience - at the moment they are receiving their largest refund of the year. Conversion timing is ideal. Conservative capture:

  • Q1 mommy-season funnel: 100-200 new email subs + 8-12 mentorship applications: $20K-$40K from mentorship rung conversions
  • Q2-Q4 high-earner funnel: 50-100 high earners through the audit: $30K-$60K from the $5K front-end conversions

Year-1 floor on this bottleneck: $50K-$100K direct, before backend rev-share rolls in.

Fix

Two parallel funnel surfaces. Shared hub. Shared mentorship landing. Different lead magnets and email sequences for the two seasons. Q1 sequence dormant in Q3-Q4. Q2-Q4 sequence dormant in Q1. The content engine (Bottleneck 4) feeds both.

Impact

You capture the Q1 mommy-season window without scrambling. You capture the Q2-Q4 high-earner window without scrambling. The compressed-timeline urgency you flagged on the WhatsApp call gets directly addressed: maximize this season and next without the funnel infrastructure being a bottleneck.

Why this is #3

The seasonality is the business. Ignoring it means losing half the year's natural conversion windows. Building it means the funnel always has the right message live.

Bottleneck 4 · LinkedIn long-form distribution to the high-earner ICP - zero presence today

Snapshot

The high-earner audience - the one that pays the $5K front-end and saves $30-50K per year in taxes - lives on LinkedIn. Business owners, C-suite, senior managers, consultants, attorneys. They search LinkedIn for tax-savings authority. You currently have no LinkedIn long-form presence as a tax operator.

This is a recurring-engagement bottleneck, not an install bottleneck. It compounds month over month once the cadence locks in. The high-earner ICP rewards depth: case-study posts, planning frameworks, "$30K saved by restructuring entity classification" specifics. The audience reads in slow scroll, comments thoughtfully, refers privately. It is the right channel for the right buyer.

Asymmetry

You have 14 years of operator stories. You have a $5K mentor-paid playbook on high-earner planning. You have an existing roster of high earners you serve. Every interaction is a teachable post. The raw material is dense. The cadence is missing.

Cost (Year-1 floor)

LinkedIn long-form for a tax-operator brand at 2 posts per week, sustained for 6-9 months, typically produces 100-300 inbound DMs from qualified high earners, 10-30 of whom convert through the $5K front-end Year-1. Conservative capture:

  • 10-20 high-earner conversions through the $5K front-end Year-1: $50K-$100K direct
  • 20% backend on those engagements over the following 12-18 months: $30K-$80K rolling
  • Compound effect of LinkedIn credibility on the mentorship cohort and top tier rungs

Year-1 floor on this bottleneck (recurring engagement): $40K-$90K in Year-1 captured revenue, plus backend that rolls beyond.

Fix

LinkedIn long-form ghostwriting cadence. 2 posts per week. Pillars: high-earner planning case studies (redacted), entity-classification frameworks, the mommy-season vs high-earner contrast, the three-vertical operator story. Content is sourced from your operator history and the $5K mentor playbook you already paid for. Repurposed across the warm-list email when appropriate.

Impact

Captures the high-earner ICP at the credibility moment. Builds inbound DM pipeline that warms straight into the $5K front-end. Compounds the brand authority across the broader category.

Why this is #4

The Install bottlenecks (1, 2, 3) need to ship first - the page where high-earner DMs land has to exist before the content engine drives DMs to it. Once the install is live, LinkedIn is the recurring engine that fills the high-earner rung.

Bottleneck 5 · Three-handle consolidation (parked Year-2 layer)

Snapshot

You operate at least three IG handles - @niriamagana (personal/legal name), @yadi_itsmyworld (lifestyle/personal-brand), @credityadi (brand/credit-facing). Each has its own audience. Each links somewhere different (or nowhere central). Audience-size on each is unverified at time of build. The consolidation problem is real regardless of per-handle count: visitors leak between handles, every handle is a different first-impression of the same operator.

Asymmetry

Even at sub-30K per handle, the combined audience-touch is meaningful. A visitor who lands on the lifestyle handle doesn't know the brand handle exists. A high earner who finds the brand handle doesn't see the lifestyle and credibility content on the others. The handles compete with themselves.

Cost (Year-1 floor)

Hard to quantify cleanly. Conservative recovery estimate: 10-15% of all cross-handle traffic gets lost without a central hub. Capturing 1-2% of recovered traffic into the seasonal lead magnets adds compounding email-list growth from day one.

Year-1 floor on this bottleneck: $5K-$25K in recovered conversions.

Fix

The hub (this build provides it) goes in the bio of all three handles. It tells visitors who you are, names each handle's role, routes warm leads to the mentorship surface, captures email for the seasonal lead magnets. You keep all three handles; the hub consolidates the funnel without forcing handle consolidation.

Impact

Recovers the leak. Turns three handles into one funnel. Compounds the brand voice across the existing handle infrastructure.

Why this is #5

Lowest direct revenue lift, highest infrastructure value. Parked deliberately behind the four moves that actually move revenue. The hub itself ships in less than a week as part of the Install package, but the consolidation play (driving real cross-handle email capture) compounds across Year-2 and beyond.

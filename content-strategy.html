Strategic frame

The content engine has two jobs:

  1. Fill the cohort + high-earner front-end during the Q2-Q4 high-earner crunch (May through December)
  2. Capture the Q1 mommy-season window (January through March)

It serves three ICPs across two seasonal cycles:

  • Aspiring credit-repair and tax-prep operators - women (mostly Latina, mostly working mothers) who want to build their own three-vertical business. IG-native. Mommy-season-aligned.
  • High earners - business owners, C-suite, attorneys, consultants who want their taxes restructured to save $30,000 to $50,000 per year. LinkedIn-native. Q2-Q4-aligned.
  • Warm-list re-engagement - the existing 600-800-person tax + credit client roster. Email-native. Year-round.

The engine does not chase mass-audience growth. It chases conversion of the right audience inside the right seasonal window.

Channel mix (priority order)

1. LinkedIn long-form · high-earner ICP (recurring engine, ranks #1 outside Q1)

The high-earner audience lives on LinkedIn. This is the recurring engagement that fills the $5K front-end rung.

  • Cadence: 2 long-form posts per week
  • Format: 800-1,200 word posts. Operator stories, planning frameworks, redacted case studies.
  • Pillars:
  • 1× High-earner case study (redacted, no client names, real savings math)

Content pillars (recurring topic buckets)

Every post fits one of six pillars. Rotate across the week.

Pillar 1 · The Three-Vertical Operator Math

What it actually costs to run tax + credit + Airbnb. Margins, client acquisition cost, the cross-sell math across the three verticals, the day-to-day. Filters tire-kickers from real prospects.

Example: "Here's what my tax practice and credit-repair book cross-sell at. The math is the offer."

Pillar 2 · The Mentee Win

Nikki anchors. As named mentees graduate and consent to public attribution, their stories layer on. Always real, named, verifiable.

Example: "Nikki started while pregnant. Two jobs. First season: $40-50K in credit repair plus $15K in taxes. She just quit one job."

Pillar 3 · The Regulatory Reality

CROA. IRS Circular 230. State tax-preparer regulations. State licensing for credit-repair organizations. The Airbnb-investing tax election. The compliance lattice that separates legitimate operators from get-rich-quick scams.

Example: "Most credit-repair courses skip CROA. Most tax-prep courses skip Circular 230. The cohort starts with both."

Pillar 4 · The High-Earner Planning Frame (LinkedIn-heavy)

Entity classification, retirement layering, depreciation, estimated-tax-planning. The $30-50K per year savings frame, taught in operator detail. This pillar lives primarily on LinkedIn.

Example LinkedIn opener: "A surgeon paying $180K in federal tax can save $42K by restructuring three things. Here's the order."

Pillar 5 · The Seasonal Pivot

Q1 mommy-season refund content. Q2 transition. Q3-Q4 high-earner urgency. The pillar that rides the calendar.

Example (Q1): "If your refund landed today and you've been thinking about starting a credit-repair business, here's the first $497 of it well spent."

Example (Q3): "If you owed more than you expected this year, your entity classification is probably wrong. Here's how to check."

Pillar 6 · The Money Mindset + Money Math

Family, kids, the Wealthy Beginnings origin frame. The bridge between operator-direct and personal. The hardest pillar to write but the one that creates parasocial trust.

Example: "Wealthy Beginnings isn't a brand name. It's what I'm building so my kids don't have to start from where I started."

CTAs (every post earns one)

Every post closes with one of three rotations:

  1. Mentorship-direct (1 in 3 posts): "Cohort applications open. Link in bio." or "If you want to build across all three, the application is at the hub."
  2. Seasonal lead magnet (1 in 3 posts): Q1: "Free Mommy Refund Checklist - link in bio." Q2-Q4: "Free High-Earner Tax Savings Audit - link in bio."
  3. Engagement (1 in 3 posts): "Comment INFO for the cohort details" or "Save this if you needed it."

Never end on no-CTA. The post is a conversion asset.

What we're not doing

  • No paid IG / Meta ads (per Yadi's stated preference on the June 3 call)
  • No mass-DM scripts (CROA + IRS Circular 230 advertising scrutiny + general spam tolerance reasons)
  • No reels-only strategy (LinkedIn long-form + carousels still convert harder for the high-ticket front-end)
  • No purchased shoutouts (cohort + high-earner audience is too specific)
  • No collab posts with non-aligned brands (manifestation coaches, get-rich-quick credit-repair "gurus", aggressive tax-loophole brands - reputational risk to the regulatory-compliance posture)

Measurement

Track three numbers, weekly:

  1. Warm-list opens + clicks (weekly email)
  2. LinkedIn inbound DMs from qualified high earners (the leading indicator for $5K front-end conversion)
  3. Cohort + high-earner applications submitted (the only conversion metrics that matter in phase one)
  4. Email subscribers across the two seasonal lead magnets (the audience-build indicator)

Ignore follower-count growth as a primary metric. A 300-subscriber email list from the right audience outperforms a 10K follower IG growth that doesn't convert.

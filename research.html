9. Compliance + niche-watch (CROA + FTC + state licensing)

This is a regulated niche. Credit repair education + income-claim marketing both sit inside FTC scrutiny zones. Every public surface in this build carries a compliance disclaimer.

Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) constraints

  • Cannot promise specific credit-score improvements (no "raise your score 100 points in 30 days" claims on public surfaces)
  • Must disclose consumer rights to dispute items themselves at no cost
  • Cannot collect payment for services not yet performed (relevant to Yadi's own business, less relevant to the mentorship - but the mentorship teaches a CROA-regulated business, so students must be educated on this)

FTC income-claim constraints

  • The verbatim "$500K in 6 months" and "$1M in 12 months" numbers from the June 3 call are SPECIFIC business goals discussed privately. Educator-not-financial-advisor framing

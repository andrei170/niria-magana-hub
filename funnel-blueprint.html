Prepared: 2026-05-13 Goal: Stand up a Wealthy-Beginnings-owned three-vertical mentorship surface (tax + credit + Airbnb) anchored on the top tier, ladder visible, two parallel seasonal funnels (Q1 mommy-season + Q2-Q4 high-earner), Skool architecture, LinkedIn distribution for the high-earner ICP. Filled in the Q3-Q4 high-earner crunch window with the Q1 mommy-season cycle compounding from there.

The offer ladder

Five visible rungs. Built around the Skool architecture you described on the WhatsApp call, spun out of the borrowed Skool into a Wealthy-Beginnings-owned standalone. Anchored on the top tier as the ceiling, with the $5K-front-and-back high-earner model as the second rung and the firm-license + monthly software as the entry. Pricing language stays high-level on public surfaces; exact dollar amounts surface only at intake.

Tier 0 · Seasonal lead magnets · Free

Two parallel lead magnets, dormant outside of season.

Q1 (January through March) - "Mommy Refund Checklist"

  • 12-page PDF + 5-email warm-up sequence
  • Promise: "What to actually do with your refund this year - the 10 moves that turn $10-13K into a second income inside 12 months."
  • Content includes: refund-deployment math, the first 5 credit-repair decisions you can fund from a refund, the LLC formation step that costs less than $300, the tax-prep cross-sell math for your own household, an introduction to the three-vertical operator path.
  • Capture point: Wealthy-Beginnings-owned landing form. Email-gated.

Q2 through Q4 - "High Earner Tax Savings Audit"

  • 8-page PDF + 3-email follow-up sequence (shorter than Q1 because the high-earner ICP wants depth not duration)
  • Promise: "The 7 entity-classification and tax-planning moves that have saved my high-earner clients $30,000 to $50,000 per year - the checklist."
  • Content includes: entity-classification decision tree (S-Corp vs LLC vs partnership), retirement-plan layering, depreciation accelerators (relevant for the Airbnb-investing vertical), estimated-tax-planning calendar, the 12-month engagement structure.
  • Capture point: Wealthy-Beginnings-owned landing form. Email-gated. Routes high-earner leads to the $5K front-end application.

Tier 0 builds the list, qualifies seriousness, and pre-warms each ICP into the right next-rung application.

Tier 1 · Software-license + monthly software · Entry rung (existing model)

Format: Monthly subscription OR one-time firm-license fee, for aspiring tax-firm operators who want their own platform.

Promise: "Launch your own tax firm under my software stack with my onboarding."

Includes: - Software access (monthly or perpetual under the one-time fee) - Firm-launch onboarding inside the mentorship community - Tax-mentee community access (3-month minimum) - The licensing and compliance walk-through that maps to the mentee's state

Pricing posture (private to intake, not on public landing): Monthly subscription rate and one-time firm-license fee anchored at the rates you described on the WhatsApp call. Public language uses ranges and tier names only.

Purpose: The entry rung for aspiring firm-owners. Monetizes them at low commitment. Captures them into your 30% rev-share alignment on filings.

Tier 2 · High-earner front-end · "Sauced Up Return" · $5K front-end + 20% backend

Format: Front-end engagement structured at a single setup fee for the planning work, with 20% backend on the tax savings produced. Scalable - your existing model.

Promise: "Save $30,000 to $50,000 in taxes this year. I do the planning, the entity work, and the implementation. You pay a setup fee. I take 20% of the savings I produce."

Includes: - Entity-classification audit - Tax plan + estimated tax plan for the year ahead - Retirement and depreciation layering - Implementation across the filing year

Purpose: Captures the high-earner ICP at the moment they realize they're overpaying. The front-end fee filters serious buyers. The backend rev-share aligns incentive.

Public landing language (compliant, no specific savings promise): "A planning engagement for high earners who want their taxes restructured. Setup fee plus performance-aligned backend. Application required."

Tier 3 · Three-vertical mentorship cohort · Anchor rung

Format: Cohort-style mentorship inside the Wealthy-Beginnings-owned Skool. Tax + credit + Airbnb curriculum. Weekly group coaching. 12-week minimum engagement.

Promise (private to applicants): "Mentor with me across tax prep, credit repair, and Airbnb investing. The full operator playbook in one room."

Public landing language: "A small cohort for operators who want to build across the three verticals: tax, credit, real-estate income. Application required."

Includes: - Weekly 90-minute group coaching calls (live, recorded) - Tax-vertical curriculum: client acquisition, the $60K-and-under tier, the high-earner planning playbook, the software stack, IRS Circular 230 compliance walk-through - Credit-repair curriculum: dispute work, CROA compliance, client acquisition without paid ads - Airbnb-investing curriculum: deal sourcing, financing, the tax treatment of the asset class (where it ties back into the tax vertical) - Private mentee community inside the Wealthy-Beginnings Skool - 30% rev-share alignment on mentees' tax filings (your existing structure - preserved)

Pricing posture: Public language uses tier names and "application required." Exact dollar amount surfaces at intake. Held at the level you described on the WhatsApp call.

Purpose: The middle anchor. The flagship rung. Where most mentees enter the three-vertical curriculum.

Tier 4 · Top tier · Private mentorship · Application gated

Format: Direct 1:1 access at the top of the ladder. Custom-scope engagement.

Promise: "For operators ready to run all three verticals at top-tier scale, with me directly inside their business."

Includes: - Direct access for the engagement window - Custom curriculum based on operator stage - Priority on the labor force (your 7 tax preppers) for mentee client work - Annual planning sessions and quarterly reviews

Pricing posture: Application-gated. Quoted at intake. Anchored at your existing top-tier ceiling (held private to intake conversation).

Purpose: The ceiling. The graduation path from the cohort. The placement for operators who arrive already running and want full-stack alignment.

Pricing rationale (private notes, never public)

Why the existing tier structure ($25K top, $5K + 20% high-earner, $99/mo or $1.5K firm-license, 30% rev-share on mentee filings) holds:

  • All rungs already validated by your existing operator state
  • The $25K top-tier ceiling is your current willingness-to-pay ceiling - holding it preserves the brand premium
  • The $5K + 20% high-earner model is your existing alignment - already running, already paying
  • The $99/mo and $1.5K firm-license rungs already convert in your existing piggyback mentorship - they capture mentees at the entry rung
  • The 30% rev-share alignment on mentee filings is the long-term margin engine - it compounds over years
  • Year-1 capture across the rungs sits in the $250K-$550K conservative range, with backend rolls pushing toward $680K over 18 months

Why none of the exact dollar amounts go on public surfaces:

  • Public-price-anchoring isn't needed at the top two tiers - application gate is the right filter
  • The Tier 1 entry rung publishes structurally ("monthly subscription or one-time firm-license fee, anchored at a working-budget tier") not numerically
  • Cold prospects who self-disqualify on price never reach the application - the gate does that work
  • Pricing flex lives at intake conversation, not at landing-page locking

VSL Preview · Niria "Yadi" Magana · Wealthy Beginnings

Prepared: 2026-05-13 Format: Short hero preview, 75-95 seconds. NOT a full 8-minute VSL. Per the locked rule: default to short previews unless explicitly asked for the long version. Placement: Top of landing page (above the lead-magnet form), embedded as a clickable poster that opens to the recorded video on a separate page or modal. Speaker: Yadi (Niria Magana), camera-direct, casual professional setting (her office at 2108 N St or a warm-lit home setup)

The 85-second script

[0:00 - 0:10] Open · Camera on Yadi · Direct

Hi, I'm Yadi.

I run Wealthy Beginnings out of Sacramento. Fourteen years in tax. Seven preppers on my team. A credit repair arm and an Airbnb arm running alongside it.

[0:10 - 0:28] The honest arc · Why this exists

Here's the truth most operators won't say out loud.

For my low ticket clients, I was good. Solid. They got everything they needed.

For my high ticket clients, I was underskilled. I knew it. So I invested in a five thousand dollar mentor to teach me what I actually needed to do at that level.

A year later, my high-earner work is structured the way it should have been from the start.

[0:28 - 0:44] The proof · The Nikki story · Walked plain

While that was happening, a woman named Nikki came to me wanting to learn what I do.

Not pay me to fix her credit. Learn the business itself.

She was pregnant. Working two jobs.

Twelve months later, she did forty to fifty thousand in credit repair, fifteen thousand in taxes, and quit one of those two jobs.

[0:44 - 0:58] The frame · The offer

So I'm passing on what I paid to learn.

Three verticals. Tax. Credit. Airbnb.

The full operator playbook I run my business on, taught the way the five thousand dollar mentor taught me.

Seven mentees already inside. Room for a small group more.

[0:58 - 1:18] The honest qualifier · Trust signal

I can't promise you Nikki's numbers.

I can promise you the same playbook I run, the same upgrade path I walked, and direct access to me through it.

If I went from competent at low ticket to capable at high ticket through a mentor and a year of work, you can run the same arc under me.

The rest is on you.

[1:18 - 1:25] CTA · Plain

If that's the kind of operator you want to be, the application's below.

Take a look.

[End at ~1:25]

Production notes

Look + feel

  • Setting: Yadi's actual office (2108 N St Ste N, Sacramento) OR a clean home-office setup with warm-tone lighting. Whatever feels native to her brand.
  • Wardrobe: Whatever she'd wear running her business. Don't dress her up for the camera. Mentorship buyers want operator-energy, not coach-on-stage.
  • Camera: Single fixed angle, eye-level, 4K if possible. No fancy cuts. Plain operator-direct talk-to-camera.
  • Audio: Lavalier mic. The voice has to sound clean and close. Trust signal.
  • Light: Soft key light from the left, fill from the right, no harsh shadows. Warm color temperature (3200K-4000K).

Edit

  • Minimal cuts. The point of the short VSL is operator-direct trust, not montage-energy.
  • One subtle b-roll insert OK if the production allows: a shot of Yadi at her desk, a shot of dispute letters on a printer, a shot of the office signage. Light touch.
  • Captions: burn-in captions in clean white sans-serif at the bottom-third. About 30-40% of viewers will watch silent first; captions are required.
  • End screen: Black card with "Apply at wealthybeginings.com" + Yadi's name + a 2-second hold. Don't overdo it.

Length

  • Target 85 seconds. Acceptable range: 75-95 seconds.
  • Anything over 2 minutes and we're back in the full-VSL territory the locked rule says to avoid.

VSL poster (the static frame on the landing page before play)

The poster image is what shows on the landing page where the VSL embeds. If Yadi doesn't ship the full video yet, the poster itself does the heavy lifting.

Poster design:

  • Background: Deep emerald (#0d3b2e) with a subtle radial gradient toward antique gold in the upper-right corner
  • Eyebrow text (top): "WEALTHY BEGINNINGS MENTORSHIP · 85-SECOND OPEN" - small caps, 11px, gold (#c9a961), 0.22em letter-spacing
  • Headline (center): Cormorant Garamond italic, "The playbook I paid to learn. Now taught directly." - The word "directly" in gold italic. 28-36px responsive.
  • Play button (center): Circular gold (#c9a961) button with a navy/emerald play triangle. 84px diameter. Soft drop-shadow.
  • Source label (bottom right): "PREVIEW · 85 SEC" - 10px, white, navy backdrop badge.

The poster has to stand alone if the video isn't recorded yet. Visitors who don't click still take the eyebrow + headline as a trust signal.

Routing

When the visitor clicks the poster:

Option A (recommended for first deploy): Click scrolls smoothly to the application form below. The poster is decorative + framing. No video plays. Sub-line says "Opt in below for the full open and the application link."

Option B (once the video is recorded): Click opens a modal with the 85-second video. End-card auto-scrolls to application form.

Default to Option A for the first deploy. Swap to Option B when Yadi records the video.

What this VSL is NOT

  • Not a sales-letter VSL with an 8-paragraph script and 12 slides of bullet points
  • Not a "let me walk you through every objection" VSL
  • Not a launch-style VSL with "you've watched, now apply" scarcity pressure
  • Not a Russell Brunson "stack-the-value" pitch

What it IS: an 85-second operator-trust signal built on the upgrade-arc story (good at low ticket, underskilled at high ticket, paid a mentor to upgrade, now teaching the same arc) plus the Nikki proof block. Converts the application-curious into application-submitted.

If the mentorship needs a longer VSL after the first intake batch, build it from real mentee-graduate footage, not from speculative pitch copy. The proof from real students always outsells the pitch.

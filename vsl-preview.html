VSL Preview · Niria "Yadi" Magana · Wealthy Beginnings

Prepared: 2026-05-13 Format: Short hero preview, 60-90 seconds. NOT a full 8-minute VSL. Per the locked rule: default to short previews unless explicitly asked for the long version. Placement: Top of landing page (above the lead-magnet form), embedded as a clickable poster that opens to the recorded video on a separate page or modal. Speaker: Yadi (Niria Magana), camera-direct, casual professional setting (her office at 2108 N St or a warm-lit home setup)

The 75-second script

[0:00 - 0:08] Open · Camera on Yadi · Direct

Hi, I'm Yadi.

I run Wealthy Beginnings, a credit repair and tax prep business out of Sacramento.

[0:08 - 0:22] The proof · The Nikki story · Walked plain

Last year, a woman named Nikki came to me wanting to learn how to do what I do.

Not pay me to fix her credit. Learn the business itself.

She was pregnant. Working two jobs.

Twelve months later, she did $40,000 to $50,000 in credit repair, $15,000 in taxes, and quit one of those two jobs.

[0:22 - 0:38] The frame · Why this exists

I'm not a coach.

I'm an operator who taught one student how to do what I do, watched it actually work, and decided to teach ten more.

So I'm opening a small beta cohort.

Ten women. Eight weeks. The full playbook I run my business on.

[0:38 - 0:55] What's in it · Clean list

Client acquisition without paid ads.

The dispute letter system.

Cross-selling tax prep to your credit repair clients.

CROA and state licensing - the boring stuff most courses skip.

Hiring your first VA.

Live group coaching with me every week.

[0:55 - 1:10] The honest qualifier · Trust signal

I can't promise you Nikki's numbers.

I can promise you the same playbook, the same templates, and direct access to me for eight weeks.

The rest is on you.

[1:10 - 1:15] CTA · Plain

If that's the kind of operator you want to be, the application's below.

Take a look.

[End at ~1:15]

Production notes

Look + feel

  • Setting: Yadi's actual office (2108 N St Ste N, Sacramento) OR a clean home-office setup with warm-tone lighting. Whatever feels native to her brand.
  • Wardrobe: Whatever she'd wear running her business. Don't dress her up for the camera. Cohort buyers want operator-energy, not coach-on-stage.
  • Camera: Single fixed angle, eye-level, 4K if possible. No fancy cuts. Plain operator-direct talk-to-camera.
  • Audio: Lavalier mic. The voice has to sound clean and close. Trust signal.
  • Light: Soft key light from the left, fill from the right, no harsh shadows. Warm color temperature (3200K-4000K).

Edit

  • Minimal cuts. The point of the short VSL is operator-direct trust, not montage-energy.
  • One subtle b-roll insert OK if the production allows: a shot of Yadi at her desk, a shot of dispute letters on a printer, a shot of the office signage. Light touch.
  • Captions: burn-in captions in clean white sans-serif at the bottom-third. About 30-40% of viewers will watch silent first; captions are required.
  • End screen: Black card with "Apply at wealthybeginings.com/cohort" + Yadi's name + a 2-second hold. Don't overdo it.

Length

  • Target 75 seconds. Acceptable range: 60-90 seconds.
  • Anything over 2 minutes and we're back in the full-VSL territory the locked rule says to avoid.

VSL poster (the static frame on the landing page before play)

The poster image is what shows on the landing page where the VSL embeds. If Yadi doesn't ship the full video yet, the poster itself does the heavy lifting.

Poster design:

  • Background: Deep emerald (#0d3b2e) with a subtle radial gradient toward antique gold in the upper-right corner
  • Eyebrow text (top): "WEALTHY BEGINNINGS COHORT · 75-SECOND OPEN" - small caps, 11px, gold (#c9a961), 0.22em letter-spacing
  • Headline (center): Cormorant Garamond italic, "The playbook I run my business on. Taught directly." - The word "directly" in gold italic. 28-36px responsive.
  • Play button (center): Circular gold (#c9a961) button with a navy/emerald play triangle. 84px diameter. Soft drop-shadow.
  • Source label (bottom right): "PREVIEW · 75 SEC" - 10px, white, navy backdrop badge.

The poster has to stand alone if the video isn't recorded yet. Visitors who don't click still take the eyebrow + headline as a trust signal.

Routing

When the visitor clicks the poster:

Option A (recommended for cohort one): Click scrolls smoothly to the application form below. The poster is decorative + framing. No video plays. Sub-line says "Opt in below for the full open and the application link."

Option B (once the video is recorded): Click opens a modal with the 75-second video. End-card auto-scrolls to application form.

Default to Option A for the first deploy. Swap to Option B when Yadi records the video.

What this VSL is NOT

  • Not a sales-letter VSL with an 8-paragraph script and 12 slides of bullet points
  • Not a "let me walk you through every objection" VSL
  • Not a launch-style VSL with "you've watched, now apply" scarcity pressure
  • Not a Russell Brunson "stack-the-value" pitch

What it IS: a 75-second operator-trust signal that converts the application-curious into application-submitted.

If the cohort needs a longer VSL after batch one, build it from real cohort-graduate footage, not from speculative pitch copy. The proof from real students always outsells the pitch.
